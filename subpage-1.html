What is a black hole?

A black hole is an object that can break up space-time. Its gravitational force is so great that it cannot emit any object, not even electromagnetic waves (light) The area in which no object can actually move out is called "event horizon", and the bright light we see in the picture is called "event horizon". There is one more thing out there, which is full of dust.,


The center of a black hole is called "singularity". It is estimated that its mass and density are infinite. Anything that passes by a black hole is attracted to its center due to its extra gravity and falls into the black hole. The more objects enter it, the more its mass increases. Black holes change the path of light. We know that when light passes through it, it never comes back, so it looks black.


But when light goes to a certain angle and passes through a certain place, it does not fall into the black hole and returns at a much larger angle. This place is near the edge of the "event horizon" and because of this it looks brighter. Due to which M7K was identified in 2019. We see many ring-like objects next to black holes in many images computer simulations. These are "attraction disks". But why are they everywhere? This is because of the space-time curvature. The amount of space-time curvature caused by the massive mass of a black hole changes the direction of light abnormally. Due to which the direction of light changes. The idea is that when a particle and an anti-particle come close to the event horizon of a black hole, one particle falls into the black hole and the other escapes, causing a loss of energy.
