LIBERATION WAR OF 1971:

Bangladesh is an independent country. This independence has come by paying the cost of a sea of blood. Bangladesh has a brief history of sacrifice,struggle and war. In the past the country was under several colonies. The country was under British colony till 1947. After getting apart from British colony and causing the birth of two different countries India and Pakistan, Bangladesh was gone under the Pakistan colony. Which was another controversial participation.

  Pakistan had two parts. East and West Pakistan. The present Bangladesh was then East Pakistan and the current Pakistan was West Pakistan. There was a difference of 1200 square mile between two Pakistan. Even after gaining the tag of a new forming country, Pakistan, this land was not free. The discrimination between two Pakistan was explicit. In every aspect of the country, East Pakistan was on the lower side. Education, Health, Military, Politics they discriminated in every sector. The most horrific example of this inequality was choosing the national language of the country. Even though almost 56% of the total population was Bengali and Bangla was their primary language, The Pakistan government had chosen Urdu as their national language. The Bengalis didn’t tolerate this discriminations. They fought back, they protested against every unequal decision of the government. But this paired country was never meant to reside. The Bengali people realised that they are not going to get their rights unless they get separation from Pakistan and form their own country. So they headed toward gaining independence from Pakistan.

 As a result, the most drastic event of Bangladesh’s history happened, a liberation war of 9 months. The brutal force of the Pakistan army was launched against the unarmed Bengalis on the midnight of 26 March,1971. They killed almost 10 thousand people on that night, took control of every important structure of the country and imprisoned the bright faces of the country. This genocide event of that night is known as “Operation Searchlight”. The Bengalis fought back. People of all ages joined the war. They sacrificed their lives for the sake of their motherland. More than 3 million people had died in this war.  Many countries and organizations of the world also stood with Bangladesh. With the support of the majority of the countries of the world and an exterminator war, Bangladesh had gained its victory against the Pakistan army on December 16,1971. No other country in the world had got their independence in such a quick time.

THE FATHER OF THE NATION BANGABANDHU SHEIKH MUJIBUR RAHMAN,

Every nation has a great leader who enlightened the path of freedom for the nation’s people. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, was a true saviour for Bangladesh. He was the pioneer of the most successful political party of Bangladesh, Awami League. From his student life, he devoted himself to the people of Bangladesh. He fought for people's rights against Pakistani government. That’s why he was always the major victim and the eye pain of Pakistani government. As a result, he had spent almost half of the Pakistan colony period in jail. During Operation Searchlight, he got arrested in the first place. But before that he had declared the independence of Bangladesh and instructed mass people to fight against Pakistani Army. The people of all ages had responded to his words and fought till gaining total freedom. After independence, he came back to the country and took the major responsibility of rebuilding this country. He was the first president of Bangladesh. In his whole life, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman had one goal, to fight for the rights of Bengali. That’s why even after a long time of his death, He is in the middle of every Bengalis heart.

Interesting facts about Bangladesh:

⧪ Bangladesh has the largest Mangrove forest, Sundarban. 

  Located in the southwestern part of Bangladesh, Sundarban is the largest halophytic mangrove   forest in the world. Thousands of different kinds of trees and animals really made Sundarban special. The Royal Bengal Tiger, national animal of Bangladesh, is definitely the chief attraction of Sundarban. Sundarban is one of the most popular tourist spots. Sundarban came under the UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1997.

⧪ Bangladesh is the reason for International Mother Language Day.

The Language Movement of 1952 is one of the most remarkable incidents in Bangladesh’s history. General people protested against the unjustified decision of denying Bangla as the national language of then Pakistan government. People of all ages and occupations had joined that movement. Even many of them had lost their lives. A language monument (known as Shaheed Minar) has been created to remember those national heroes who laid down their lives on that day. UNESCO had declared 21st February as International Mother Language Day as a remembrance of this day in November,1999.

⧪ Bangladesh has the longest female lead government.

Bangladesh is the only country in the world where the leader of the government is a female for more than two decades. The current prime minister, Sheikh Hasina, is ruling the country for more that 16 years in her two terms of election(1996-2000 & 2008-onward). The speaker of the parliament is also a female, Shirin Sharmin Chaudhury. Furthermore the chairperson of the opposition party is a female as well, Rowshan Ershad.

⧪ Bangladesh has the longest unbroken sea beach in the world, Cox’s Bazar(120km).

Located in the southeastern part of Bangladesh, Cox’s Bazaar is one of the major highlights of Bangladesh. It is the most popular tourist destination of the  country. Cox's Bazar has spectacular scenic beauty. The soothing sound of the sea waves splashing on the shore as soon as you arrive in Cox's Bazar you will feel welcomed in this beautiful land.

⧪ The world’s largest delta found in Bangladesh, The Delta of Ganga River.

The Ganges Brahmaputra delta lies both in India and Bangladesh. It has a surface area of 100 000 sq km. This delta has created a strong effect on the socio-economic policy of the country. As the people of this subcontinent are heavily dependent on agriculture, this delta system plays a vital role in this regard. Sweet water of this delta has blessed the country with a variety of fishes and crops.

⧪ The climate of Bangladesh is tropical.

Bangladesh is the only country where you will find 6 different seasons. The weather is never so catastrophic here. The Himalaya protects the country from heavy snowfall, and cold air comes from Siberia. As the country is obsessed with a lot of rivers, there is a balance between rain and hot weather. The geographical location of this country is a blessing and a curse at the same time. As the country is situated at the meet point of 3 tectonic plates, there is a high chance of a severe earthquake. Most parts of the landsight are only 10 meters higher from sea level. Natural calamities like flood, drought, tsunami,etc are so common here.  

⧪ The Bengalis have really unique food culture.

Food is the biggest part of a nation’s culture. It represents the taste and choice of a nation. Because of the geographic location and mindset of people, there is a lot of spice in Bengali food culture. The foods are of different colors and smell. As the country has an extensive amount of fish resources, most of the foods are fish oriented. There is a proverb that says “Mache Vaate Bangali”. Which means Bengali people are all about fish and rice. There are some traditional foods like Panta Elish which represent various cultural festivals.

⧪  Dhaka is known as the “City of Mosque”.

As the majority of the people are of muslim religion, mosque is the most common structure of the city. There are at least 1-2 mosques at a 1km range. In urban areas it can rise up to 3-5 km range. At the center of the city, there is a mosque on every corner. The decorations in the mosques are so attractive. Many mosques have stood for decades which bear the brief history of the city.

⧪  Dhaka University is known as the “Oxford of East”.

Dhaka university is the most reputed university of the country. It has a huge contribution in the development of not only Bangladesh but also the whole South Asia. This university was established in 1921 and recently celebrated its 100 years anniversary. The students and teachers of this university have made a huge contribution in digitaliging the country. They also have played an enormous part in the liberation war of  1971.

⧪ There are a lot of Bengalis who live outside of the country.

The most valuable aspect of Bangladesh is definitely its people. A lot of Bangladeshis are expatriate people. They serve a great purpose in the economical nomenclature of the country. Remittance is one of the main prospects of Bangladesh's economy. The growth of remittance has significantly increased in recent days. Which of course has created a positive impact on the economic development of the county.

⧪ Dhaka is the most densely populated city on Earth.

Because of being the industrial,political and economical centre of the country, people are heavily dependent on the capital which has made Dhaka the most densely populated city of the world. Everyday a lot of people step on Dhaka to change their fortune. Almost 25 thousand people per square kilometer live in Dhaka. In total Dhaka has a population of 21 million.

⧪ Dhaka has a street with more people than a village, Shankhari Bazar-Old Dhaka.

As we already know Dhaka is the most densely populated city in the world,Shankhari Bazaar is one of the oldest areas in Old Dhaka. It is one of Dhaka’s famous streets where almost 10000 people live. There are a number of temples as almost 70% of the residents are hindus. The road is pretty narrow like most of the roads of Old Dhaka. This road was built during the late mughal period.

⧪ The traffic of Dhaka in insane.

Want to try your level of patience, welcome to Dhaka. As it is one of the busiest cities in the world, Dhaka has a huge amount of traffic violence which is such a curse for the city.

⧪ Village Baniachong is the largest village in the world.

Baniachong is an upazila of Sylhet division. The huge area of ​​32.43 square miles is considered as a symbol of tradition to the people. About 1 lakh 15 thousand people live in Baniachong village consisting of 120 mahallas.

People’s Culture :

The major highlight of Bangladesh is definitely its people. The Bengalis are really welcoming. There are people of mainly Muslim and Hindu religion. But one can’t identify who is what religion to the naked eye. Because the dress culture is pretty much the same for all religions. 

The main greeting is for the Muslims “Assalamu Alaikum” and for Hindus “Nomoskar” in Bangla. However, one can play it safe by just saying “Hello”. The majority of the people speak in Bangla. Even the name of billboards and transports are also in Bangla. Numbers are written in Bengali script as well.

English is not widely spoken in Bangladesh, especially outside of the city. The young generation is more familiar with English but older people have some issues with it.

Bangladesh is famous for its waterways. These waterways connect the whole country like a wave.

Bengalis are very passionate about fish. As it is a riverine country, the living of a large percentage of people depends on fish. People are typically non-vegetarians. Fish curry and vegetables are the most common dishes here.

Bangladesh is a riverine country and most of its land is fertile. The country is blessed with so much natural resources which has created an impact on the choice of their occupation. Not surprisingly almost 50% of people are farmers and agriculture is their main occupation. Bangladesh has a huge variety of crops and the productivity cost is so low. The country produces a lot of crops every year which meets the demand of the country. Even many crops get exported every year. Rice is the staple food of the country so most of the farmers actually rely on cultivating paddy.

The dress code of Bengali people is like most other Asian nations. Mens wear normal jeans and t-shirts and women wear salwar kameez. The traditional dress for men is Lungi and Fatua, for women it is Saree.

CULTURAL FEST:

Cultural fests represent the core of a nation's culture. The best way to know about a nation is observing their cultural fest. The Bengalis have got several cultural festivals. These fests have been taming the hearts of people for decades. Many of these fests are religion oriented. The major cultural fests are,

➤  Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha.

Eid is the biggest festival for Bengali people. As most of the people are of Islam religion, Eid is considered as a dignified festival. The most mentionable aspect of Eid is even though Eid is a Muslim bound festival, People of all religion join this fest. That’s the cultural harmony among the people of Bangladesh.

➤ Durga Puja

Like Eid, Durga Puja is another religious festival which is highly fascinating among the people of Bangladesh. People of other religions along with Hindu religion celebrate this fest.

➤ Pohela Boishakh

Pohela Boishakh is the biggest cultural fest in Bangladesh. It is the new year eve of bengali calendar. People observe this day with great obsession. They wear traditional dress, sing the traditional song of Pohela Boishakh (Esho He Boishakh) and gather together in Ramna Park from early dawn. Panta Ilish (moisten rice with Hilsa fry and curry) is the traditional platter on Pohela Boishakh. In a word, this festival conveys every aspect Bengali culture has.

➤ National Martyrs Day

National Martyrs day is a significant day on the remembrance of those valiant heroes who fought for the right of Mother Language in the Language Movement of 1952.

➤ Independence Day

Independence day is the most precious day for a nation. After all the struggles and the historic war of 1971, the country had gained its independence. To commemorate this significant glory, the nation celebrates 26 March as Independence day.

➤ Victory Day

After the war of independence, Bangladesh got its independence from Pakistan on December 16,1971. Therefore, this remarkable day is observed as Victory day every year.

➤ Rabindra Jayanti

Rabindranath Tagore is the writer of the national anthem of Bangladesh. He is one of the delegate poets of Bengal. Rabindra Jayanti is an annually celebrated cultural festival in the remembrance of Rabindranath Tagore’s birthday anniversary on May 9 every year.

➤ Nazrul Jayanti

Kazi Nazrul Islam is the national poet of Bangladesh. He is also the writer of the war anthem of Bangladesh. Nazrul Jayanti is another annually celebrated cultural fest in the remembrance of Kazi Nazrul Islam’s birthday on May 24 every year.

➤ Nobanna

Bangladesh is an agricultural country. Paddy is the main crop people cultivate here. The lives of a huge number of people are dependent on this. Nobanna is the time of threshing the paddy. After the hard work of the whole year, farmers can finally take the crops and sell them. That’s why they arrange different cultural activities and celebrate Nobanna. Pitha (a kind of sweet) is the biggest attraction of Nobanna. The farmer's family bake these sweets using rice  flour,molasses,coconut,etc.

➤ Boshonto Utshob

Bangladesh is a country of six different seasons. In each season, nature decorates herself with different entities. Of all the seasons, spring is the best. The natural beauty of spring is so attractive and charming. That’s why people welcome this season with glory. They wear colorful clothes (yellow saree and punjabi mostly) and attend various cultural movements.

➤ Shakrain

Shakrain is one of the oldest cultural fests oriented mostly in Puran Dhaka. It is also known as the kite festival (Ghuri Utshob in Bangla). It is an Annual fest observed with flying kites. Generally it occurs at the end of Bangla month Poush (14-15 January). local people organizes A huge arrangement of lighting and music. This fest is so popular among the young generation. Thousands of people come together and fly kites in the sky and enjoy their time with family and friends.

➤ Nauka Baich

Nauka Baich is a popular festival in the rural area of the country. It has been a popular sport from the ancient period. During the rainy season when all the rivers of the country are full of water, people compete in this boat race competition with each other. They make their team and make colorful and attractive boats. The boats are typically very long. People sing sari gaan (local folk song) as they compete during the race.

➤ Joy Bangla Concert

The Joy Bangla Concert is the biggest concert for youths commemorating the historical speech of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman on March 7,1971. It is an annual event helds at the Army stadium every year. This concert made its debut in 2015. After that, it has become a part of the country’s pop culture. This concert stitches the wartime patriotic melodies of Bangladesh with the western-influenced rock genre.

TRADITIONAL BENGALI FOODS:

The Bengalis have a huge variety in food culture. Food is a passion for Bengalis. A lot of spice and color will definitely trigger the taste bud of a person. Start the day with traditional shahi paratha with egg omelet and fries. Rice items are staple foods here. Foods like kachchi biryani and bhuna khichuri are dishes on deal here. One must try these foods to feel the Bengali food culture. For dessert, there are foods like Rasmalai (a special sweet full of milk and sugar syrup), Sweet yoghurt,Borhani(a kind of spicy drink).

Pitha holds a big part of Bengali food tradition. There are several types of pitha like vapa,puli,chitoi,nokshi,etc. Winter is the perfect monsoon to make these pithas. Most of these pithas are made of rice flour,molasses,coconut,milk,butter and so on. Bangladesh is really famous for its street foods like most of the South Asian countries. These foods are so spicy and peppery. Some biting street foods are: velpuri,fuchka,jhal muri,chanachur,Dosa,etc. There are some traditional platters like Panta Ilish as well.

HIGHLIGHTS OF BANGLADESH :

Bangladesh has a mix up of both forged and natural beauty. These instances carry the dignity,beauty and history of this country. Some of the popular place and illustrations are,

  1. Highlight of Old Dhaka    

         Old Dhaka is the most ancient and historical place of Bangladesh. Some structures of this                city are of the 18th century.  One must enjoy a trip to Old Dhaka and explore the city by Rickshaw.

  1. Trip to Sreemongal.

Sreemongal is the tea capital of Bangladesh. Bangladesh is one of the biggest tea manufacturers and exporter countries of the world. For tea lovers, Sreemongal is like heaven. There are some of the largest tea gardens in the country. The natural scenery and environment is definitely a peaceful memory.

  1. Shat Gambuj Mosque

Shat Gambuj mosque is an ancient and historic mosque. This mosque is certified by UNESCO as a world heritage site. The major highlish about this mosque is its unique structure and 60 dome-shaped pillars.

  1. Sabash Bangladesh Monument

The Sabash Bangladesh Monument is an incarnation to pay tribute to the valiant fighters of 1971. It is located at Rajshahi University premises. Nitun Kundu is the designer of this sculpture.

  1. Ahsan Manjil

Ahsan Manjil is the palace of Dhaka's Emperors. It is located in Old Dhaka. It stands with the glory of the old Nawabs of Bengal.

  1. Shilpakala Academy Art Gallery

It is the National Art Gallery of Bangladesh. The academy became the sole conservationists and protectors of art in 2003 and founded the National Art Gallery.

  1. Saint Martin

Saint Martin is the only Coral Island of the country. St. Martin is generally known as “Narikel Zinzira” in Bengali, means 'Coconut Island' and this is the only coral reef island in Bangladesh. It is a small island in the north eastern part of the Bay of Bengal, created in the southernmost part of our country. The scenic beauty of Saint Martin is its main attraction for all.
